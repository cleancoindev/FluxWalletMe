FluxWallet Wrapped ETC (FWETC) is an ERC20 token minted and destroyed at a 1:1 ratio, representing the ETC held within the FWETC Smart Contract.
Simply put, it gives ETC the powers of an ERC20 token - programmable, multi-functional - but still most importantly, immutable, decentralised and secure.

There's two parts to how FWETC works: Wrapping ETC and Unwrapping FWETC. Let's quickly go through both.

Wrapping ETC
  • 1. You deposit ETC into the FWETC Smart Contract, via this Dapp interface
  • 2. Once transactions are confirmed, you receive FWETC, minted 1:1 with the ETC you deposited
  • 3. You use the FWETC however applicable to your requirement
  • 4. You come back here to unwrap your FWETC once you are done

Unwrapping FWETC
  • 1. You unwrap FWETC tokens via this Dapp interface
  • 2. FWETC tokens are destroyed from the Current Supply, at a 1:1 ratio with the ETC you receive from unwrapping the FWETC tokens.
  • 3. You have ETC in your wallet, ready for regular use or trading!

FluxWallet Wrapped ETC is made available for use by the general public, via this Dapp interface.
Right now, FWETC is not listed on any exchanges or DEXs - however, You are welcome to wrap and unwrap ETC as you need / see fit.
